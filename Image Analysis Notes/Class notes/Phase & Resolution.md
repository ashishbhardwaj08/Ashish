### Phase Information

**Phase information** represents the positional or structural information of an image in the frequency domain.

It is useful for:  
• determining the **location of objects** in an image  
• preserving **edges and shapes**  
• calculating **depth information** in images (e.g., stereo vision, holography, interferometry)

Phase contains most of the **geometrical and structural details** of an image, while magnitude contains mainly **intensity information**.

Loss of phase information results in loss of image structure even if magnitude is preserved.

---

### Image Resolution and Error

**Image resolution** refers to the ability of an image to distinguish small details.

It is of two types:

1. **Spatial Resolution**  
    → smallest detectable detail in an image  
    → depends on pixel size and sampling rate
    
2. **Gray Level Resolution**  
    → smallest detectable change in gray level  
    → depends on the number of intensity levels (bit depth)
